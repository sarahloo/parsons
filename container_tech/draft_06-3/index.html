Container technologies

Referencing Sofia’s (2000) paper on container technologies, this study of containers in my dormitory room focuses on containment through the lens of mobility. This is in both the sense that the containers themselves have been collected through various means; and that container technologies have facilitated my own mobility – allowing me to relocate to New York City and (re-)create a ‘facilitating environment’ of my own, by servicing needs ranging from physical necessity to social connection.

The filter categories aim to call attention to this movement of goods and information, and the technologies that make possible this supply, demand and re-sourcing. We invest significant time and effort in acquiring things, only to discard them when we no longer have use for them – sometimes even before then – or space to contain them. Would this change if we were more aware of our transience of our existence and finiteness of the earth’s resources? What would we choose to take with us or leave behind?

REFERENCES
Sofia, Z. (2000). Container technologies. Hypatia, 15(2), 181-201.
